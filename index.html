Tetris

Gameplay

Tetris is a very simple game.
The goal is to last as long as possible without
reaching the top of the playing field.
The player can acheive this by maneuvering differently-shaped
pieces known as tetrominoes.
As the player aligns these pieces into lines, they dissapear.
If the player cannot align the pieces, they will stack to the top of
the playing field and make the player lose the game.


History

In 1979, Alexey Pajitnov was working at the Soviet Academy of Sciences.
While he was working there, he enjoyed creating puzzle
games inspired by games from his childhood.
The computer he developed Tetris on had no graphical interface,
so the earliest version of it was made up of ASCII characters.
It quickly spread around his workplace like wildfire and
soon became popular in every place with a computer in Moscow.
Years later, after intense copyright disputes, it became wildly popular around the world.

Tetrominoes

There are seven different tetrominoes, each of them made of four 'blocks'.
They are all named after letters in the English alphabet.

  The "I" piece.

  The "O" piece.

  The "T" piece.

  The "J" piece.

  The "L" piece.

  The "S" piece.

  The "Z" piece.
